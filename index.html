Meet the Librarian

The Heart Behind the Shelves

Hi, I'm Brandi Rowton — and honestly, this library is one of my favorite places on earth. I'm a proud wife to Dalton, mama to four incredible kids (ages 15, 12, 7, and 5), a member of the Delight City Council, and an active part of my church community in Blevins. Life is full and I love every bit of it.

I came to this role with years of experience as a librarian's aide — cataloging, shelving, circulation, you name it — but more than anything, I came with a genuine love for people. I believe a small-town library can be so much more than a place to check out books. It can be a hub, a haven, and a place where everyone from the tiniest toddler to the oldest neighbor feels right at home.

My passion is fostering a love of literacy, creating programs that bring the community together, and making sure every single person who walks through that door feels welcomed and cared for. Whether you need help finding your next favorite book, a quiet place to sit, or just a friendly face — I'm here for it.

— Brandi Rowton

Librarian, Delight Public Library

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

A few things folks often ask us.

What are your hours? +
We're open Tuesday through Friday, 9:30 AM to 4:30 PM. We close for lunch from 12:00–1:00 PM. We are closed on Saturdays, Sundays, and Mondays.
Is there a fee to get a library card? +
Library cards are free for residents of the Delight community. Stop in during our hours and Brandi will get you set up!
Do I need to be inside to use the WiFi? +
Our free WiFi is available 24/7 — including from our parking lot. The password is a secret, but come visit and I'll tell you maybe 👀
Do you have books for young children? +
Absolutely! We carry books for every age — from board books for babies all the way through middle grade, young adult, and adult titles.
When is the summer reading program? +
Our summer program runs each summer for school-age children. Check our Facebook page or call us for this year's dates and details!
Can I use the computer without a library card? +
Yes! Our public computer is available to everyone in the community. Just stop in during open hours and ask Brandi for help getting started.
